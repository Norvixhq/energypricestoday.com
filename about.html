About EnergyPricesToday

Modern energy intelligence, built for clarity. EnergyPricesToday.com delivers live oil and gas prices, state-by-state pump data, geopolitical analysis, and corporate intelligence across the global energy sector — fast, readable, and completely free. No paywalls, no clutter, no 90-second video auto-plays. Just the energy information you need, the way it should work.

Energy Matters. The Information Shouldn’t Be Hard to Find.

Energy is the most consequential sector on earth. It moves more capital, affects more consumers, and responds to more geopolitical forces than any other industry. Yet energy information has become harder to access, not easier. Legacy trade publications hide behind $5,000 subscriptions. General news sites bury commodity data under ads and video interstitials. Aggregators repost without context. Social media surfaces noise, not signal.

EnergyPricesToday.com was built to fix this. We deliver live benchmark prices, curated breaking news, state-by-state gas data from AAA, weekly Baker Hughes rig counts, and independent analysis of the forces shaping oil, gas, power, and the global energy transition. All free. All readable on any device. All structured so you can find what you need in seconds.

We believe energy coverage should feel modern. Clean design. Fast load times. Clear hierarchy. Accurate data. Honest attribution. That is the standard we build to.

Data Sources

Every data point on EnergyPricesToday.com is sourced from recognized authorities. Commodity prices (WTI, Brent, natural gas, refined products) update every 5 minutes via OilPriceAPI using real CME/ICE exchange data. Gas prices come from the AAA Daily Fuel Gauge Report, updated daily across all 50 states plus D.C. Rig counts are sourced from Baker Hughes North America (weekly) and International (monthly) reports. Stock prices for U.S.-listed energy companies update via the Finnhub API. Breaking news headlines are pulled from the Google News RSS feed and major publishers.

Where data is reference-based (OPEC blends, regional crudes, international prices), we label it clearly. We never pass off stale data as live, and we never invent prices.

Editorial Process

Original analysis on EnergyPricesToday.com is researched from primary sources (EIA, IEA, Baker Hughes, SEC filings, government announcements, company press releases) and major reporting outlets. We aim for accuracy over speed. When facts change, we update. When we make errors, we correct them openly and note what changed.

We do not take payment for coverage. We do not run sponsored articles disguised as analysis. We do not let advertising pressure affect editorial decisions.

Corrections & Updates

Prices and news update continuously throughout the day. Article content is updated when material facts change — especially in rapidly developing situations like the current U.S.-Iran conflict.
