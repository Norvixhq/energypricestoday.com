Understanding Crude Oil Prices

Crude oil prices are influenced by a complex web of factors including OPEC+ production decisions, global demand growth, geopolitical tensions, inventory levels, refining capacity, and currency movements. WTI (West Texas Intermediate) is the primary U.S. benchmark, while Brent crude serves as the global reference price. The spread between these benchmarks reflects transportation costs, quality differences, and regional supply-demand dynamics.

Oil futures contracts trade on exchanges like NYMEX and ICE, with front-month contracts serving as the most-watched price indicators. Prices shown here represent the most recent settlement or spot prices and may be delayed.
