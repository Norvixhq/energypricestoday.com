Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between WTI and Brent crude oil?
WTI is the primary U.S. benchmark delivered at Cushing, Oklahoma. Brent is the global benchmark pricing ~75% of internationally traded crude. Brent typically trades $2-5 higher due to its seaborne nature.
Why do some prices show delays?
Primary benchmarks update every 5 minutes via live API (marked LIVE). Regional and OPEC blends are reported through different channels with delays of 1-30 days.
What determines crude oil prices?
Global supply-demand balance, OPEC+ production decisions, U.S. shale output, geopolitical risks (Strait of Hormuz, Red Sea), inventory levels, refinery utilization, and macroeconomic conditions.
How do oil prices affect gas prices?
Crude oil accounts for ~55% of retail gasoline cost. A $10/barrel increase typically adds $0.25-0.30/gallon at the pump within 1-2 weeks.
What is backwardation vs contango?
Backwardation: near-term futures higher than longer-dated contracts (tight supply signal). Contango: future prices exceed current prices (oversupply signal).
How often are prices updated on this page?
Nine benchmarks marked LIVE update every 5 minutes via OilPriceAPI using real CME/ICE exchange data. All other prices are reference data with delay labels per section.
