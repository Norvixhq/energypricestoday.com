U.S. energy markets are navigating an unusually volatile stretch. After Friday's brief reopening and 11% crude crash, Iran has re-closed the Strait of Hormuz, the waterway that carries roughly 20% of global seaborne oil, and IRGC gunboats have fired on Indian-flagged tankers. WTI has whipsawed back upward on the reversal, with the Brent-WTI spread widening as shipping uncertainty returns.

At the pump, the AAA national average has fallen to $4.058 — a seventh consecutive daily decline driven by the earlier crude drop working through refiner margins. Prices still vary sharply by state, with California at $5.83 and Oklahoma at $3.41, reflecting regional fuel specs, refinery capacity, and tax structure.

The immediate market question is whether the U.S.-Iran ceasefire holds past its Tuesday, April 21 expiration. Pakistan's FM Dar is mediating ahead of a second Islamabad round. Separately, an Israel-Lebanon 10-day ceasefire is being tested after a French UN peacekeeper was killed.
