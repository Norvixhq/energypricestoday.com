ExxonMobil Q1 Earnings Expected to Benefit from Elevated Crude Prices

StaffApr 16, 20265 min read

ExxonMobil is expected to benefit significantly from Q1's elevated crude price environment when it reports earnings in early May, with WTI averaging near $100 per barrel during the quarter.

The integrated supermajor's diversified portfolio — upstream production, refining, chemicals, and low-carbon solutions — provides insulation across market cycles. Permian production continues to grow following the Pioneer Natural Resources acquisition.

Exxon has maintained its capital discipline focus, returning approximately 40-50% of operating cash flow to shareholders via dividends and buybacks. The company has raised its dividend for 40+ consecutive years.

CEO Darren Woods has guided toward 4.2 million boe/d production by 2027, with the Permian Basin and Guyana providing primary growth vectors.
