Ceasefire Expires April 22 With No Extension in Sight

April 12, 2026 — Ceasefire Expires April 22 With No Extension in Sight.

10 Days Until Ceasefire Expires

The two-week ceasefire between the United States and Iran, brokered by Pakistan and announced on April 8, 2026, is set to expire on April 22 with no visible path to extension. The collapse of 21-hour marathon negotiations in Islamabad on April 12 has eliminated the primary diplomatic mechanism that could have converted the temporary truce into a lasting peace framework.

Market analysts warn that the expiration represents the most dangerous inflection point since the conflict began on February 28. Goldman Sachs has projected that WTI crude could reach $130-150 per barrel if hostilities resume at full scale, while even a partial resumption focused on naval operations in the Strait of Hormuz would likely push prices above $120.

Pakistan's Foreign Minister Ishaq Dar has urged both sides to maintain the ceasefire regardless of the negotiation outcome, calling it "imperative for regional stability." However, Iran's IRGC has signaled that without progress on its core demands — sanctions relief, recognition of its nuclear rights, and an end to Israeli operations in Lebanon — the truce has no foundation for continuation.

The United States faces its own constraints. President Trump has described the war as already won while simultaneously threatening a "full naval blockade" — a contradictory posture that leaves markets uncertain about America's actual intentions. Vice President Vance's departure from Islamabad without a deal was described by CNN as a "fundamental blow to nascent hopes of finding an off-ramp to this crisis."
