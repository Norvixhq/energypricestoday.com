Live Updates: Iran war pushes oil prices to 4-year high as Hegseth set to face fresh round of questioning

Staff Apr 30, 2026 5 min read

Crude oil benchmarks have climbed to their highest levels since 2020 as geopolitical tensions in the Middle East intensify concerns over supply disruption. The Strait of Hormuz — through which roughly one-third of seaborne oil trades — remains a focal point for traders assessing downside risk to global crude flows. Escalating Iranian tensions have reignited hedging demand, supporting both WTI and Brent crude.

Energy markets are pricing in a widening risk premium tied to potential disruptions along key shipping lanes and production hubs across the region. Traders are monitoring whether any supply outages materialize or whether geopolitical rhetoric translates into actual export constraints. Historical precedent shows that even the threat of Iranian supply loss can move oil futures meaningfully higher.

On the political front, confirmation hearings for key administration nominees continue in Washington, with energy policy implications under review. While Pete Hegseth faces fresh questioning on Capitol Hill, energy markets remain focused on how U.S. policy toward Iran sanctions and Middle East engagement may evolve. Any shifts in these positions could influence crude supply expectations and pricing.

Crude traders are balancing near-term supply concerns against longer-term demand signals from major economies. The current price environment reflects heightened uncertainty rather than confirmed supply losses, leaving room for volatility in either direction. Market participants are watching for official statements from OPEC+, U.S. officials, and Iranian authorities to clarify the trajectory of regional tensions.
