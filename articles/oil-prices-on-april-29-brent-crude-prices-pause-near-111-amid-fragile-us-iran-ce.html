Oil Prices On April 29: Brent Crude Prices Pause Near $111 Amid Fragile US-Iran Ceasefire Talks

Staff Apr 29, 2026 5 min read

Brent crude held steady near the $111 per barrel level on April 29 as market participants weighed the implications of ongoing diplomatic efforts between the United States and Iran. The absence of sharp price movement reflects cautious optimism tempered by uncertainty about whether talks will yield tangible results. Traders remain focused on any developments that could affect crude supply from the Middle East’s largest producer.

Geopolitical risk premiums have long underpinned oil valuations, particularly given Iran’s nuclear program and its proximity to critical shipping lanes. A successful ceasefire arrangement could theoretically ease tensions and reduce the “risk premium” baked into current prices. However, the fragility of such negotiations means the market is unlikely to make bold directional moves until there is concrete evidence of progress.

The broader energy complex has also remained relatively stable, with attention divided between Middle East geopolitics and global demand signals. Economic data from major consuming regions, particularly Europe and Asia, continues to influence crude valuations alongside traditional supply-side factors. The OPEC+ production framework and its adherence to announced quotas remain structural supports for current price levels.

Investors should monitor official statements from negotiating parties and international bodies for clarity on ceasefire status and duration. Any sudden escalation in rhetoric or military activity could destabilize the current price equilibrium, while formal agreements could create downward pressure. The energy market’s ability to price in diplomatic progress or deterioration will likely remain a key driver through the coming weeks.
