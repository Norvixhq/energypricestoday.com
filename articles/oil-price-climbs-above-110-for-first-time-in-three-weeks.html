Oil price climbs above $110 for first time in three weeks

Staff Apr 29, 2026 5 min read

West Texas Intermediate crude broke through the $110/barrel level on Monday, marking the strongest performance for U.S. benchmark oil in approximately three weeks. The move reflects renewed demand signals and supply tightness in global markets, offsetting earlier weakness that had pushed prices lower in recent trading sessions. Brent crude, the international standard, also climbed in sympathy with WTI.

Traders attributed the rally to tighter inventories and ongoing geopolitical concerns affecting supply routes. The Strait of Hormuz, through which roughly one-fifth of global seaborne oil passes daily, remains a focal point for market participants monitoring regional tensions. Any disruption to flows through critical chokepoints typically supports crude values across both benchmarks.

OPEC+ production decisions continue to influence price direction as the cartel balances output targets against global economic uncertainty. The organization’s commitment to output management has helped underpin prices during periods of demand weakness. Compliance levels among member states remain a key variable for market observers assessing near-term support levels.

Energy traders are now watching for confirmation that crude can sustain levels above $110, as the breach comes after weeks of consolidation in lower ranges. Technical levels and upcoming macroeconomic data will likely guide volatility in the sessions ahead. Any surprise in inventory reports or demand indicators could test the durability of the current rally.
