Oil price climbs above $110 for first time in three weeks

Staff Apr 29, 2026 5 min read

West Texas Intermediate crude pushed into triple-digit territory this week, marking the first breach above $110/barrel since late October. The move reflects a combination of supply tightness and renewed concerns about global demand resilience. Trading volume has remained steady as investors reassess their near-term outlook for the energy complex.

OPEC+ production decisions and compliance rates continue to anchor market sentiment, with the cartel’s ongoing output management underpinning price floors. Any disruption signals — whether from geopolitical tensions or unexpected maintenance windows — tend to trigger rapid repricing in the crude market. This dynamic keeps traders vigilant for headline risk in key producing regions.

Refinery utilization rates across the major demand centers have held firm, supporting crude intake expectations heading into the winter season. Seasonal factors typically boost heating oil demand in the Northern Hemisphere, which can provide additional support for prices if supply remains constrained. LNG export schedules and petrochemical feedstock demand also factor into the current calculus.

The climb above $110 suggests market participants have moved past some of the recession concerns that weighed on prices in recent weeks. However, macroeconomic headwinds remain relevant, and any sharp slowdown in manufacturing or trade could quickly test support levels below. Traders will continue monitoring central bank policy, dollar strength, and production updates from OPEC+ members for directional cues.
