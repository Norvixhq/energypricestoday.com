Oil price briefly hits $120 after reports of 'extended' Iran blockade

Staff Apr 29, 2026 5 min read

Crude futures spiked to $120 per barrel on reports suggesting Iran may extend restrictions on shipping through its territorial waters, rattling markets already sensitive to Middle East supply concerns. The intraday move reflects trader anxiety over potential disruptions to one of the world’s most critical oil transit routes. WTI and Brent both registered sharp gains during the session, though neither settled at the spike level.

The Strait of Hormuz remains the focal point for geopolitical oil risk, with roughly one-third of global seaborne crude passing through its narrow channel daily. Any credible threat to transit through Iranian waters can trigger immediate price reactions, as downstream buyers fear delays or rerouting costs. Previous incidents in the region have demonstrated how quickly risk premiums can materialize in futures markets.

Traders are monitoring official statements from Iranian authorities and shipping operators to clarify the scope and duration of any announced restrictions. Supply fundamentals remain tight enough that even temporary disruptions can amplify price volatility. The market’s reaction underscores how quickly sentiment shifts when Middle East tensions intersect with global energy demand.

The brief spike reflects both technical buying and genuine concern about supply security, though longer-term oil prices depend on OPEC+ output decisions, global economic growth, and the duration of any actual shipping constraints. Energy traders are expected to watch for confirmation of the blockade’s specifics and likely timeline over coming hours. For now, the $120 level marks an important tactical level to monitor in the near term.
