JPMorgan Says Oil Prices Still Have Further to Rise

Staff Apr 24, 2026 5 min read

JPMorgan’s energy analysts have signaled that crude markets remain poised for upward movement despite recent volatility. The bank’s outlook reflects persistent supply-demand imbalances and structural factors that continue to support prices above current levels. This assessment aligns with broader market expectations that geopolitical risks and production constraints will keep pressure on crude benchmarks.

OPEC+ production management remains a key pillar in JPMorgan’s bull case for oil. The cartel’s ongoing output discipline, combined with voluntary production cuts extended through 2025, limits supply growth at a time when global demand remains resilient. Any further compliance from member states would reinforce the tightening dynamics that underpin higher valuations.

Refinery utilization rates and seasonal demand patterns are also factoring into the upside scenario. Refiners are operating at elevated rates ahead of summer driving season in key markets, which historically supports crude offtake. Additionally, maintenance schedules and potential supply disruptions from geopolitical flashpoints could further constrain the available barrels.

JPMorgan’s positioning suggests investors and traders should monitor inventory levels, dollar strength, and production updates from OPEC+ members closely. Any unexpected tightness in physical markets or fresh supply constraints could accelerate the timing of additional price appreciation. Market participants should remain alert to both bullish catalysts and downside risks as the outlook evolves.
