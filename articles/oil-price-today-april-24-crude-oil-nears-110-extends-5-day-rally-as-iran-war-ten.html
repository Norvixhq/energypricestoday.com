Oil Price Today (April 24): Crude oil nears $110, extends 5-day rally as Iran war tensions rise. Here’s wh

Staff Apr 24, 2026 5 min read

Crude oil has extended its fifth consecutive day of gains, approaching the $110 per barrel level as geopolitical risk premiums drive market sentiment. The rally reflects heightened concerns over regional stability in the Middle East, a critical hub for global petroleum supply. Traders are pricing in the potential for supply disruptions should tensions escalate further.

Iran’s escalating rhetoric and military posturing have reignited concerns about chokepoint vulnerabilities, particularly the Strait of Hormuz, through which roughly one-fifth of globally traded oil passes daily. Any material disruption to flows through the waterway would immediately tighten global balances and support prices at current elevated levels. Market participants are closely monitoring official statements and military movements for signs of direct confrontation.

On the macro front, crude remains supported by OPEC+ production discipline and steady demand from Asian refiners despite global growth concerns. The five-day rally has reversed some of the weakness seen in early April, though prices remain below the peaks seen earlier in 2024. Technical support levels are holding as momentum builds on geopolitical headlines.

Investors should monitor developments from Tehran and Washington closely in coming sessions, as policy announcements or military developments could trigger sharp intraday moves. The weekly EIA inventory report and official OPEC+ communications will also influence price direction. Energy traders are pricing elevated volatility around near-term risk events in the region.
