Oil prices today: Crude falls below $100 as Trump announces two-week ceasefire with Iran

Staff Apr 26, 2026 5 min read

West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude dropped below the $100 per barrel level on news of a two-week ceasefire agreement between the United States and Iran. The announcement eased immediate concerns about potential disruptions to regional oil supply and shipping through the Strait of Hormuz, a critical chokepoint handling roughly one-third of seaborne crude. Market participants have been pricing in geopolitical risk premiums for months, and the de-escalation signal triggered a sharp sell-off in energy futures.

A ceasefire period reduces the likelihood of Iranian retaliatory strikes or broader military escalation in the near term, both of which could have disrupted production or threatened tanker traffic in the Persian Gulf. Traders quickly unwound some of the hedges they’d accumulated during elevated tensions. The move reflects a classic response in commodity markets: lower perceived threat equals lower risk premium.

The breakthrough comes at a delicate time for crude valuations, with global economic data mixed and OPEC+ production decisions under constant scrutiny. Two weeks is a short window, however, and investors remain cautious about whether diplomatic progress will hold beyond the initial period. Any breakdown in talks could quickly reverse today’s losses and reignite supply concerns.

Energy traders will be watching for any updates on the ceasefire terms and whether broader negotiations on sanctions relief or nuclear issues gain traction. The fundamental supply-demand backdrop—including refinery utilization rates and seasonal demand patterns—will continue to anchor price levels once the initial geopolitical reaction settles. For now, the shift toward de-escalation has handed bulls a significant setback.
