Oil prices today: Crude falls below $100 as Trump announces two-week ceasefire with Iran

Staff Apr 29, 2026 5 min read

Crude oil benchmarks retreated below the $100-per-barrel threshold following an announcement of a temporary ceasefire agreement between the United States and Iran. The two-week pause in hostilities eased geopolitical risk premiums that had supported prices in recent sessions. Market participants swiftly repriced expectations for supply disruption in one of the world’s most critical oil-producing regions.

The Strait of Hormuz, through which roughly one-fifth of globally traded petroleum passes daily, had been a focal point for traders concerned about potential Iranian retaliation or U.S. military action. A de-escalation reduces immediate fears of supply interruptions from the Persian Gulf, allowing energy markets to refocus on fundamentals including demand signals and inventory levels. Brent and WTI crude both moved lower as safe-haven buying reversed.

The ceasefire remains fragile and limited in duration, leaving longer-term geopolitical uncertainty intact. Traders will monitor whether negotiations extend beyond the two-week window or whether tensions resurface once the temporary pause expires. Any breakdown in talks could quickly reverse today’s price weakness.

Downstream markets showed modest support from lower crude costs, though refining margins remain pressured by weak seasonal demand. The broader energy complex is now watching inventory reports and OPEC+ production decisions for the next significant price driver. Oil’s move below $100 has shifted focus back to supply-demand balances rather than geopolitical headlines.
