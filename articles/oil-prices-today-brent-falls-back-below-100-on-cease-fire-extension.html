Oil Prices Today: Brent Falls Back Below $100 on Cease-Fire Extension.

Staff Apr 22, 2026 5 min read

Crude oil retreated from the $100-per-barrel threshold following news of an extended cease-fire agreement, signaling reduced immediate geopolitical risk to global supply chains. The move reflects market participants’ willingness to step back from elevated risk premiums that had supported prices in recent weeks. Brent crude, the global benchmark, slipped below the psychologically significant level as traders reassessed supply vulnerability in the near term.

Geopolitical tensions have historically underpinned oil price floors, particularly when regional conflicts threaten chokepoints like the Strait of Hormuz or major producing nations. The cease-fire extension appears to have eased concerns about supply disruption, at least temporarily, allowing fundamentals to reassert influence over the market. Traders will continue monitoring developments closely, as any escalation could quickly reverse the day’s losses.

The decline also reflects broader market dynamics, including global demand signals and inventory levels across key trading hubs. A pullback from triple-digit pricing can encourage marginal demand while potentially pressuring smaller producers reliant on higher price environments. OPEC+ production decisions and voluntary output adjustments will remain critical factors supporting prices at current levels.

Energy markets remain sensitive to headline risk, meaning the cease-fire extension may prove temporary in its price impact if geopolitical conditions shift. Traders are watching inventory data, refinery utilization rates, and any statements from major oil-producing nations for guidance on directional momentum. For now, the sub-$100 level suggests the market is pricing in a lower-risk scenario—though vigilance remains warranted.
