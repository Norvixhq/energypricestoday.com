Oil price rises above $120 after reports of 'extended' Iran blockade

Staff Apr 30, 2026 5 min read

Crude oil futures climbed above the $120 per barrel threshold on Monday following reports that Iran may maintain restrictions on shipping through critical waterways for an extended period. Market participants cited supply concerns as the primary driver of the intraday move, with traders reassessing geopolitical risk premiums across both WTI and Brent benchmarks. The move marks a notable shift in price action after weeks of consolidation in the $110–$118 range.

The Strait of Hormuz handles roughly one-third of seaborne crude oil trade globally, making any disruption a significant concern for energy markets. Reports of “extended” restrictions, if confirmed, could reduce available transport routes and force regional buyers to seek alternatives or pay shipping premiums. Traders are monitoring official statements from Iranian authorities and international maritime bodies for clarity on duration and scope.

OPEC+ production levels remain a secondary factor in today’s rally, though the cartel’s compliance with its output agreement continues to influence longer-term price expectations. Refining margins in the Gulf have tightened in response to potential supply tightness, signaling that downstream operators are adjusting their purchasing strategies. The broader energy complex, including natural gas and refined products, has not shown comparable strength, suggesting the move is specific to crude supply dynamics.

Analysts caution that headline-driven rallies above $120 often lack staying power without fundamental demand signals or additional supply shocks. Upcoming weekly inventory data and comments from major oil exporters will be watched closely to determine whether this price level can hold. Market participants should expect continued volatility until the scope and duration of any Iranian measures become clearer.
