Oil Prices Today: Crude Falls Below $100 as Two Ships Turn Back From U.S. Blockade in Hormuz Strait

Staff Apr 20, 2026 5 min read

West Texas Intermediate crude dipped below the $100 per barrel threshold today, marking a retreat from recent highs amid renewed concerns over shipping disruptions in the Strait of Hormuz. Two vessels diverted their routes away from the strategically vital waterway, citing heightened security risks in the region. The move underscores ongoing tensions that have periodically pressured energy markets over the past several months.

The Strait of Hormuz remains one of the world’s most critical chokepoints, with roughly one-fifth of global oil trade flowing through its narrow passage. Any sustained disruption to shipping traffic can trigger immediate supply concerns and price volatility. Today’s vessel diversions, while limited in scope, reinforced investor caution and contributed to downward pressure on crude valuations.

Energy traders continue to balance competing forces: weakness in crude demand signals and economic headwinds on one side, against geopolitical risk premiums in the Strait on the other. The sub-$100 level suggests bearish sentiment is currently outweighing supply-side concerns, though that dynamic could shift quickly if maritime incidents escalate. OPEC+ production decisions and U.S. inventory data remain key focal points for the coming week.

Market participants will be watching for further shipping incidents and any official statements from regional authorities regarding Strait safety. The reversal of two cargo vessels is unlikely to cause material supply shortages on its own, but it signals heightened caution among operators. Crude prices remain sensitive to both logistics developments and macroeconomic data in the days ahead.
