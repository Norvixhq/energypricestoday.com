Brent crude oil prices top $100 as US awaits Iran's response on peace talks

Staff Apr 25, 2026 5 min read

Oil benchmarks strengthened on geopolitical uncertainty as markets assessed the implications of stalled nuclear diplomacy in the Middle East. Brent crude’s move above the $100 per barrel threshold reflects trader concerns over potential supply disruptions should negotiations deteriorate further. Risk premiums have widened on the back of regional tensions that could affect flows through the Strait of Hormuz, a critical chokepoint for global crude shipments.

The energy market’s sensitivity to Iran-related news underscores how quickly sentiment can shift in an already tight market. Any escalation in tensions could constrain supply from one of OPEC’s largest producers, whose output remains well below pre-sanction levels. Conversely, a breakthrough in talks could ease geopolitical risk and potentially pressure prices lower in the near term.

U.S. crude—which typically trades at a discount to Brent—has also firmed alongside international benchmarks, though the spread between the two grades reflects domestic refining demand and export dynamics. Traders are closely monitoring official statements from Washington and Tehran for any signals about the pace and tone of negotiations. The waiting period itself has created price volatility, with risk assets sensitive to headline developments.

Analysts note that sustained prices above $100 could begin to moderate demand growth, particularly in price-sensitive sectors like transportation and chemicals. The outcome of these diplomatic talks will likely remain a key market driver in coming weeks, with both bulls and bears watching for concrete progress or setbacks. Energy markets will continue pricing in elevated geopolitical risk until clarity emerges on the direction of talks.
