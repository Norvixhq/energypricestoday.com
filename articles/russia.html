Russia — Urals Crude, Sanctions, Asian Pivot

Russia is the world's second-largest oil exporter and the largest natural gas exporter. Since 2022, sanctions have restructured Russian energy flows: crude oil redirected to Asia at discounted prices, while Europe pivoted away from Russian pipeline gas.

Current Context

Urals crude trades at a substantial discount to Brent, with Indian and Chinese refiners absorbing most of the displaced volume. The Druzhba pipeline serves Hungary and Slovakia under sanctions exemptions. Recent ceasefire negotiations have raised the prospect of partial sanctions relief.
