Oil Prices Rise After Trump Warns ‘No More Mr Nice Guy’ in Iran War, OPEC Fractures

Staff Apr 30, 2026 5 min read

Crude oil futures climbed in early trading Thursday following heightened rhetoric from U.S. leadership regarding Iran policy. The shift in tone signals a potential shift in Washington’s approach to Tehran, raising concerns among traders about geopolitical risk premiums in energy markets. Markets have already priced in some degree of volatility, though the full extent of any policy change remains uncertain.

The Strait of Hormuz, through which roughly one-fifth of global oil passes daily, remains a focal point for investors monitoring supply chain risks. Any escalation in U.S.–Iran tensions could disrupt flows from the Persian Gulf, affecting both crude availability and refining operations across Asia and Europe. Traders are actively reassessing their positioning ahead of potential developments.

Meanwhile, unity within OPEC+ continues to fracture, complicating the cartel’s ability to manage global supplies. Recent production decisions have exposed disagreements between major members, limiting their collective leverage over prices. The combination of internal division and external geopolitical pressure has left crude markets in a state of heightened uncertainty.

Energy markets will likely remain volatile as both political developments and OPEC+ cohesion unfold over coming weeks. Investors are monitoring official statements and diplomatic channels for clarity on the trajectory of U.S. policy, while also watching for any coordinated supply responses from the cartel. Volatility itself may persist until a clearer picture emerges.
