Oil prices plunge as Iran says Strait of Hormuz 'open' during ceasefire

Staff Apr 19, 2026 5 min read

Crude benchmarks fell sharply Tuesday following Iranian assurances that the Strait of Hormuz would remain navigable during an announced ceasefire. The statement from Tehran eased concerns about potential supply disruptions through one of the world’s most critical petroleum chokepoints, through which roughly one-third of globally traded seaborne crude passes daily. Market participants had priced in a risk premium ahead of the announcement, anticipating possible restrictions on shipping traffic.

The Strait of Hormuz has long been a focal point for oil market volatility, given its geopolitical sensitivity and the volume of exports it handles from the Persian Gulf region. Any credible threat to its operation typically triggers sharp rallies in WTI and Brent crude, as traders rush to cover supply-disruption scenarios. Iran’s commitment to keep the waterway open during the ceasefire period removes a significant tail risk that had weighed on sentiment.

Energy traders are now reassessing the supply outlook for the medium term, with the reduced geopolitical premium allowing prices to reflect underlying fundamentals more clearly. Refinery runs, OPEC+ production decisions, and global demand indicators will likely reassert themselves as primary price drivers in the coming weeks. The relative calm in the Strait also provides relief to shipping operators and importers who had factored in elevated transit costs.

Investors should monitor the durability of the ceasefire and any statements from Iranian officials regarding future operations in the region, as reversals in tone could quickly reinstate volatility. The energy market’s reaction underscores how geopolitical assurances can rapidly shift sentiment when supply chains depend on stable passage through contested waters. The coming days will test whether this relief rally reflects genuine de-escalation or merely a tactical pause.
