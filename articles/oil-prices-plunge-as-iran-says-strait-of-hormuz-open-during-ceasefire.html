Oil prices plunge as Iran says Strait of Hormuz 'open' during ceasefire

Staff Apr 19, 2026 5 min read

Crude oil futures fell sharply following Iranian officials’ reassurance that the Strait of Hormuz would remain open during an active ceasefire. The statement reduced geopolitical risk premium that had been built into global benchmarks ahead of potential regional escalation. Markets had priced in supply disruption concerns tied to one of the world’s most critical chokepoints, through which roughly one-fifth of global petroleum passes daily.

The Strait of Hormuz has long been a flashpoint for oil market volatility, with any threat to transit triggering swift repricing across WTI and Brent contracts. Iran’s explicit commitment to keeping the waterway accessible during the ceasefire period signals reduced immediate tension in the region. Traders shifted focus away from geopolitical hedging and back toward fundamentals, including inventory levels and demand signals.

Energy markets typically respond to supply continuity assurances by normalizing prices toward levels supported by production balances and macroeconomic conditions. The reduction in geopolitical risk suggests market participants believe the ceasefire terms will hold at least through the near term. However, long-term uncertainty around regional stability remains a factor that could re-enter pricing if conditions deteriorate.

Downstream markets, including refining spreads and physical crude premiums, may stabilize as supply route concerns ease. Participants will continue monitoring Iranian statements and regional developments for any indication that the ceasefire terms are under pressure. The next key data points will include OPEC+ production reports and any shifts in military posturing that could signal renewed tension.
