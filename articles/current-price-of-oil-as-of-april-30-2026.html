Current price of oil as of April 30, 2026

Staff Apr 30, 2026 5 min read

Crude oil markets are trading within established ranges as of late April 2026, reflecting the interplay of supply management, seasonal demand patterns, and geopolitical considerations that characterize the current energy landscape. Both WTI and Brent benchmarks continue to respond to the usual drivers: OPEC+ production decisions, U.S. inventory reports, and expectations around refining activity heading into the peak summer driving season.

Supply-side dynamics remain anchored by OPEC+ compliance with agreed production targets, while non-OPEC producers including the United States and Canada contribute significant volumes to global markets. Recent weeks have seen the typical spring maintenance cycle at refineries and production facilities, which influences both crude demand and inventory levels across major consuming regions.

Demand seasonality is a key factor heading into May, with refiners traditionally increasing runs to meet higher gasoline and diesel consumption during warmer months. The transportation and power-generation sectors are beginning to show the uptick in activity typical of late spring in the Northern Hemisphere, which supports crude offtake expectations.

Market participants are monitoring geopolitical developments, OPEC+ meeting schedules, and macroeconomic indicators that could influence price direction in the weeks ahead. Any significant shifts in supply availability or demand forecasts will likely be reflected in crude valuations as summer approaches and trading patterns evolve.
