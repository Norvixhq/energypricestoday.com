Oil prices plunge and stocks jump after Trump announces conditional ceasefire with Iran

Staff Apr 28, 2026 5 min read

Global oil markets reacted sharply to news of conditional ceasefire negotiations between Washington and Tehran, with crude prices falling as geopolitical risk premiums unwound. The announcement eased immediate concerns about potential attacks on Middle Eastern infrastructure, particularly in the Strait of Hormuz—a critical chokepoint for roughly one-fifth of global oil exports. Equities surged on the prospect of de-escalation and improved economic outlook, reflecting investor appetite for risk-on positioning.

The ceasefire conditions remain in focus for oil traders, as their specifics will determine whether de-escalation holds and supply chains normalize. Any agreement reducing tensions could allow crude inventories to stabilize and refiners to operate without elevated hedging costs. However, market participants are monitoring whether either party might use negotiation delays as leverage, which could reinstate volatility.

Energy markets have priced in heightened geopolitical risk over recent weeks amid escalating regional tensions. A durable ceasefire could reverse some of those embedded premiums, particularly if it opens pathways for dialogue on broader sanctions relief or nuclear negotiations. Conversely, any breakdown in talks would likely trigger a sharp reversal in today’s gains.

Refiners and downstream operators may benefit from lower crude costs and reduced insurance premiums on shipping through contested waters. LNG markets could also experience pressure as lower oil prices typically correlate with cheaper gas contracts globally. The energy sector’s full response will depend on durability and detail of the actual agreement terms once formally announced.
