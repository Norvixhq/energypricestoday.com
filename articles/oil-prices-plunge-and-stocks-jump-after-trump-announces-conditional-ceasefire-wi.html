Oil prices plunge and stocks jump after Trump announces conditional ceasefire with Iran

Staff Apr 29, 2026 5 min read

Global energy markets reacted sharply to diplomatic developments in the Middle East on Tuesday, with crude benchmarks extending losses amid reduced geopolitical risk premiums. The announcement of a conditional ceasefire framework between Washington and Tehran signaled a potential de-escalation of tensions that have underpinned oil valuations for months. Equity indices surged on the news, reflecting broader investor appetite for risk assets when geopolitical flashpoints ease.

Oil’s retreat reflects the unwinding of risk premium that has persisted since previous escalations in the Persian Gulf region. WTI and Brent crude had been supported by concerns over potential disruptions to shipping through the Strait of Hormuz, a chokepoint through which roughly one-fifth of global crude passes daily. With tensions appearing to moderate, traders reassessed supply-security assumptions that had buttressed prices.

The conditional nature of the ceasefire agreement adds a layer of caution to near-term market positioning. Energy traders and investors will likely monitor compliance timelines and implementation details closely, as any deterioration in negotiations could quickly reverse the current risk-off sentiment. Market volatility may persist until concrete steps confirm a durable diplomatic resolution.

Broader market movements suggest investors are rotating toward equities on improved sentiment around Middle East stability and potential interest-rate implications. Oil-linked sectors and energy stocks experienced mixed performance, as lower crude prices weigh on producer valuations even as geopolitical risk recedes. Refinery margins and demand-side dynamics will remain key variables as markets digest the longer-term implications of reduced regional tension.
