Oil price soars to $119 after reports of 'extended' Iran blockade

Staff Apr 29, 2026 5 min read

Crude oil jumped sharply in early trading following reports that Iran may implement an extended blockade affecting regional shipping. WTI crude surged past $119 per barrel, reflecting immediate market concern over potential supply disruptions through one of the world’s most critical energy chokepoints. Brent crude tracked higher in tandem, as traders reassessed geopolitical risk premiums across the complex.

The Strait of Hormuz remains vital to global energy security, with roughly one-third of all seaborne traded oil passing through its narrow waters each day. Any sustained disruption—whether through military action, sanctions enforcement, or blockade—would create immediate pressure on supplies, particularly for Asian and European refineries reliant on Middle Eastern crude. Market participants have grown accustomed to occasional friction in the region, but reports of an “extended” timeline signal a more prolonged concern than typical incidents.

Energy traders are monitoring official statements and diplomatic channels for clarity on the blockade’s scope and duration. If confirmed as genuinely extended, the move could trigger strategic reserve releases by consuming nations and accelerate interest in alternative supply routes. Oil majors and refiners with exposure to Persian Gulf production are already adjusting hedging positions and assessing inventory strategies.

The price spike underscores how geopolitical events in the Middle East continue to carry outsized weight in global energy markets. Investors and operators will likely remain on high alert until there is greater certainty about the actual implementation and economic impact of any blockade measures. Fundamentals in supply and demand remain secondary to risk sentiment in the near term.
