Trump Warns He May Not Extend Ceasefire, Threatens to Resume Bombing Iran

StaffApril 18, 20265 min read

Coverage of trump warns he may not extend ceasefire, threatens to resume bombing iran. Iran’s April 18 re-closure of the Strait of Hormuz reshapes the energy risk landscape. With the U.S.-Iran ceasefire expiring Tuesday April 21, traders and governments are watching closely.

Roughly 20% of global seaborne oil normally transits the strait. Iranian Revolutionary Guard forces have warned that approaching vessels will be treated as “cooperating with the enemy.” Gunboats have fired on Indian-flagged ships and a container vessel was hit by a projectile off Oman.

Trump aboard Air Force One Friday night: “Maybe I won’t extend it, so you’ll have a blockade and unfortunately we’ll have to start dropping bombs again.” Pakistan is mediating a second round of negotiations next week in Islamabad.

AAA national gas average is at $4.058 — down 7.7 cents week-over-week. Monday’s crude trading will set direction for pump prices over the coming days. Shell, Cheniere, and Aramco are adjusting operations based on the reopened uncertainty.
