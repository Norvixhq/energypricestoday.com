Oil prices today: Crude climbs as US-Iran talks uncertainty keeps markets on edge; Brent tops $106

Staff Apr 25, 2026 5 min read

Crude benchmarks extended gains today as geopolitical tensions surrounding U.S.-Iran negotiations weighed on investor sentiment. Brent crude traded above $106 per barrel, while West Texas Intermediate followed suit, reflecting broad risk premium in the market. The uptick underscores how diplomatic uncertainty continues to influence energy markets, particularly when regional stability in the Middle East remains in question.

Talks between Washington and Tehran have produced little concrete progress in recent weeks, leaving traders to price in the risk of sustained supply disruptions. Any escalation in tensions could threaten flows through critical chokepoints like the Strait of Hormuz, which handles roughly one-third of global seaborne oil trade. Market participants are closely watching official statements and back-channel communications for signs of either progress or further deterioration.

Supply-side dynamics also support current price levels, though OPEC+ output decisions and compliance rates continue to shape longer-term expectations. Production data from key members remains closely watched, as do any signals about future coordination on output management. Refinery runs in the U.S. and Europe are holding firm, maintaining baseline demand even amid macroeconomic headwinds.

Investors should monitor diplomatic developments in the coming days, as any material shift in U.S.-Iran relations could trigger sharp volatility in crude prices. Energy traders are also keeping an eye on the weekly rig count and inventory data, both of which provide additional clues about supply and demand balance. The combination of geopolitical risk and fundamental market mechanics suggests crude prices may remain range-bound unless clarity emerges from the negotiation process.
