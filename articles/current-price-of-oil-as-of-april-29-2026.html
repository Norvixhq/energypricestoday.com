Current price of oil as of April 29, 2026

Staff Apr 30, 2026 5 min read

Crude oil markets closed the trading session on April 29, 2026, reflecting ongoing dynamics in global supply and demand. Traders continued to monitor geopolitical developments, seasonal demand patterns, and inventory levels as key drivers of near-term price direction. The energy complex remains sensitive to shifts in economic growth expectations across major consuming regions.

OPEC+ production decisions and compliance rates remain central to price support in the current environment. Member states’ adherence to agreed quotas continues to influence market sentiment, while non-OPEC supply additions compete for market share. Refiners and consumers are adjusting their purchasing strategies in response to evolving crude quality preferences and logistics constraints.

The physical crude market showed typical regional basis differentials, with light and heavy grades trading at varying spreads depending on refinery demand and shipping logistics. Spot markets in the Atlantic Basin and Asian hubs reflected local supply-demand conditions, while storage utilization rates influenced forward curve dynamics. Financial traders and hedgers remained active across futures and options markets.

Broader economic indicators, including manufacturing activity and transportation demand, will likely drive oil sentiment in the coming weeks. Seasonal summer driving season considerations in the Northern Hemisphere are beginning to influence expectations. Market participants are watching for any shifts in central bank policy that could impact global growth and energy consumption trends.
