Gasoline could drop below $4 in coming days

Staff Apr 19, 2026 5 min read

Crude oil prices have retreated sharply in recent trading sessions, pulling down retail fuel costs across the United States. The decline reflects a combination of softer demand signals, rising inventory levels, and easing supply concerns that had supported higher prices earlier this summer. Downstream gasoline futures have tracked crude lower, creating conditions where average pump prices could fall decisively below the $4 threshold in major markets within days.

Current WTI crude trades near levels last seen in early summer, a shift that follows weeks of geopolitical premium and production tightness. Refinery throughput remains solid, and gasoline inventories have begun rebuilding as demand seasonally weakens heading into autumn. Without a fresh supply shock or renewed geopolitical risk, the momentum favors continued downward pressure on wholesale crack spreads and retail margins.

Regional variation will likely persist, with coastal markets typically seeing sharper declines than inland hubs due to product availability and logistics. Stations in states with lower state fuel taxes and those supplied by well-stocked terminals could reach sub-$4 levels first. Drivers should monitor local price trends, as convenience store markups and station competition also affect how quickly refinery cost savings translate to the pump.

Energy traders and market watchers will be watching this week’s weekly petroleum inventory data for confirmation that supply-demand dynamics remain loose. Any significant drop in refinery runs or unexpected spike in demand could pause the decline, though current fundamentals suggest the path of least resistance remains downward. For consumers, the shift underscores how quickly energy markets can reverse when multiple headwinds align.
