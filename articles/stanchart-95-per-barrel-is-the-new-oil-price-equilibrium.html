StanChart $95 Per Barrel Is The New Oil Price Equilibrium

Staff Apr 23, 2026 5 min read

Standard Chartered’s assessment of oil market fundamentals has shifted toward a more stable mid-range valuation, reflecting broader consensus among traders and analysts that crude has found a sustainable level after months of volatility. The bank’s view suggests that current supply-demand dynamics, geopolitical tensions, and macroeconomic headwinds have begun to settle into a predictable range. This marks a departure from the wild swings that characterized oil markets through much of the past year, when recession fears and OPEC+ production decisions created outsized uncertainty.

Several factors appear to be anchoring prices at this level. Demand destruction from higher interest rates has offset some supply concerns, while OPEC+ production management continues to prevent a glut. Refinery utilization remains moderate across key regions, and inventories have stabilized near five-year averages in the U.S. and other developed markets.

Geopolitical risks—including potential disruptions in the Middle East and ongoing sanctions on Russian crude—remain priced into the market rather than creating fresh premium spikes. This suggests traders believe current tensions are unlikely to escalate into major supply losses that would push prices materially higher. The Strait of Hormuz and other chokepoints retain their importance, but the threat premium appears contained.

Whether this equilibrium holds depends largely on whether economic growth remains subdued without tipping into severe recession. Energy traders will continue watching central bank policy and corporate earnings for signals of demand recovery or decline. A sustained move away from this level would likely require either a major disruption to supply or a significant shift in global growth expectations.
