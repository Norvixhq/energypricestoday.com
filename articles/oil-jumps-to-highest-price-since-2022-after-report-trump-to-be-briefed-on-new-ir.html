Oil jumps to highest price since 2022 after report Trump to be briefed on new Iran options

Staff Apr 30, 2026 5 min read

Crude prices rose sharply on reports that the incoming administration is preparing new policy options regarding Iran, a major geopolitical risk factor for global oil markets. The move reflects investor concern that any change in U.S. posture toward Tehran could disrupt crude supplies or trigger regional tensions. Iran is OPEC’s third-largest producer, and tensions in the Persian Gulf directly influence pricing across both WTI and Brent benchmarks.

Market participants have been monitoring U.S.–Iran relations closely since the recent escalation of regional tensions. Any significant policy shift has the potential to affect crude exports from the world’s fourth-largest oil producer and disrupt shipping through the Strait of Hormuz, through which roughly one-third of globally traded seaborne oil passes. Traders are pricing in added geopolitical premium as uncertainty around diplomatic and military options remains elevated.

The energy complex has been volatile in recent weeks, driven by OPEC+ production decisions, demand concerns, and Middle East developments. A sustained briefing on Iran policy signals that energy markets may face renewed geopolitical volatility in the near term. Refiners and traders are adjusting hedging strategies in response to the shifting risk landscape.

Investors are watching for additional clarity on the scope and timing of any potential policy changes. Energy markets have historically reacted sharply to Iran-related headlines, given the country’s role as a significant crude supplier and its position in a strategic chokepoint. Sustained oil strength will depend on whether new initiatives move toward confrontation or if diplomatic channels remain open.
